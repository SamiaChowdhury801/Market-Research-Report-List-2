<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The residential occupancy sensors market comprises several types, each featuring unique technology for detecting occupancy. PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors detect body heat by sensing infrared radiation, making them energy-efficient for passive monitoring. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to identify movement, effectively covering larger areas. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies to enhance accuracy and reduce false alarms. Other types may include microwave sensors or photoelectric sensors, which offer varied applications and capabilities in home automation and energy management.</p></p>
