<p><p>Commercial sound masking systems are categorized into two main types: networked and non-networked systems. Networked systems use digital technology and interconnected devices to control and adjust sound masking levels remotely, allowing for versatile management across multiple areas. These systems are ideal for large spaces or buildings. Non-networked systems, on the other hand, operate independently, typically requiring manual adjustments on each unit. They are often more straightforward and cost-effective for smaller environments where centralized control is less critical.</p></p>
