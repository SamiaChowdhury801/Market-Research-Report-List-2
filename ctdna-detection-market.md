<p><p>The ctDNA detection market is categorized into two main types: independent detection and joint detection. Independent detection involves analyzing ctDNA as a standalone biomarker to identify mutations or alterations specific to tumors. This method focuses solely on ctDNA for diagnostics. In contrast, joint detection combines ctDNA analysis with other biomarkers, such as traditional tissue biopsies or other molecular markers, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and providing a more comprehensive view of cancer progression and treatment response.</p></p>
