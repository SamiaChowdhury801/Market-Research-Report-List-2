<p><p>The biomedical cryopreservation equipment market includes various types designed for specific temperature requirements. Medical refrigerators maintain temperatures between 2℃ and 8℃ for storing pharmaceuticals and biological samples. Blood refrigerators, calibrated at 4℃ ± 1℃, are used for blood storage. Medical freezers operate between -10℃ and -25℃ for preserving sensitive materials. Medical cryopreservation boxes provide temperatures from -10℃ to -50℃, while medical cryogenic storage boxes function at -50℃ to -150℃. Liquid nitrogen tanks store samples at ultra-low temperatures of -150℃ to -196℃. Additional equipment may also cater to specialized needs.</p></p>
